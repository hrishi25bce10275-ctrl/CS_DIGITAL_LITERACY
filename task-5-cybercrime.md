# Case Study: Phishing Attack on College Student

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted entities to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or personal data. It typically occurs through deceptive emails, messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Bait**: Victim receives an urgent email pretending to be from their bank, college, or a popular service like Google, claiming account issues or prize winnings.
2. **Hook**: Email contains alarming language ("Account suspended!") and a link to a fake login page mimicking the real site.
3. **Deception**: Clicking the link directs to a spoofed website with identical branding. Victim enters login credentials or financial info.
4. **Harvest**: Stolen data is captured by attackers in real-time.
5. **Exploitation**: Credentials used for account takeover, fund transfers, or sold on dark web.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

College students are prime targets due to limited cybersecurity awareness, frequent use of free Wi-Fi, and handling stipend money via UPI. Attackers exploit busy schedules and trust in familiar brands.

## Real Incident Example

In 2023, a VIT student lost ₹45,000 when a phishing email posing as SBI urged "account verification." The link led to a fake site capturing UPI PIN, enabling fraudulent transfers.

## Consequences

- **Financial Loss**: Immediate theft of savings or loan money.
- **Identity Theft**: Long-term damage from stolen personal data.
- **Academic Impact**: Stress, time loss resolving issues during exams.
- **Reputation Harm**: Compromised college email used for further scams.
- **Legal Issues**: Potential liability if stolen accounts used for crimes.
